I do agree that the format isn’t perfect yet. I still like these nested packages, because it separates the different parts of the application (and I don’t see why plain numbers are any better than an actual structure? ;) ).
When I started the repository, my goal was to
(1) bring together all the different implementations
(2) agree on a common denominator.

We have to finish (1) first before we actually can concentrate on (2) though.

I suppose it makes sense to have the different parts of the application and have the different implementations side-by-side within that package. Then we can decide how we compose the ‘final’ specifications of that part.

While we still work on the very 1.0 specifications, isn’t it favourable to stick to named proposals, instead of throwing around with numbers? (even though I like the “BOLT” names…)
